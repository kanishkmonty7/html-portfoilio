RECIPE FOR BUTTER PANEER

Ingredients:

  • 250g paneer (Indian cottage cheese), cubed
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
  • 1/2 cup cream
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Prepare Paneer: Heat a tablespoon of oil in a pan and lightly fry the paneer cubes until they turn golden brown. Remove from heat and soak them in warm water for 15-20 minutes. This step helps to keep the paneer soft.
  2. Make the Gravy: In the same pan, add another tablespoon of oil and butter. Once heated, add chopped onions and saut√© until they turn translucent.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook until the raw aroma disappears.
  4. Pour in the tomato puree and cook until the mixture thickens and the oil separates from the sides.
  5. Spice it Up: Add red chili powder, turmeric powder, coriander powder, and garam masala. Stir well and cook for a couple of minutes.
  6. Blend it Smooth: Once the mixture is cooked and the spices are well incorporated, let it cool slightly. Then, blend it into a smooth paste using a blender or food processor.
  7. Finish the Dish: Transfer the blended mixture back to the pan. Add kasuri methi and salt. Stir well.
  8. Pour in the cream and mix until well combined. Let it simmer for a few minutes.
  9. Add Paneer: Drain the paneer cubes from water and add them to the gravy. Gently mix them in, making sure not to break the paneer.
  10. Final Touch: Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and a drizzle of cream if desired.
  11. Serve: Serve hot with naan, rice, or your choice of Indian bread.

Enjoy your delicious butter paneer!
